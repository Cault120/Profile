A paragraph about me

I have ADHD. What this means to most people is that I can’t focus nor can I sit still for five minutes.Whereas that isn’t all that far off, the truth is that it's a complicated thing to describe. It is a disorder that acts more like a list of symptoms that don’t seem to make much sense. That is mostly because it affects everyonejust a little differently. That means that I can find someone who has few if any shared symptoms or experiences. It also means that finding treatments or aid to help out is nigh impossible for some. How do you treat getting bored with something after five minutes, or the reverse, being obsessed with something for no reason? It's not like we can just push past and focus on the task at hand when every ten seconds there is a new thing in your vision or ear that sparks a rabbit hole of thought that you don't realize you are in until fifteen minutes later. The best is when people say that they have ADHD when they very clearly don’t and have no idea how much it messes with your brain. Most of it is little things that just seem stupid at the time. It's looking back on all of them to realize that this is how you live your days, second guessing where you put your keys after distinctly remembering them being right here on the counter no more than twenty minutes prior then finding them alreadyin your pocket because you subconsiously picked them up to fiddle with them while finding your phone. Though I will say that some of the things we come up with at four in the morning while going down yet another Alice in Wonderland spiral of things we can't begin to describe make for some great conversations with friends. Anyways, have fun and don’t forget to wear your seatbelt.
